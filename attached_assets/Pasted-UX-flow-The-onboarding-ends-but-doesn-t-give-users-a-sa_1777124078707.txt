UX & flow

The onboarding ends but doesn't give users a satisfying "you're all set" moment — a brief welcome screen with their name and a prompt to write their first testimony would increase early engagement significantly
The Search page could add filters (by category, date, most-amened) so users can actually find what they're looking for rather than just keyword searching
When a user posts a testimony, they're likely dropped back somewhere without any celebration — a success moment ("Your testimony has been shared!") with a share button would feel more rewarding
Profile page

There's no way for other users to follow or connect with people whose testimonies they find meaningful — right now the community feels anonymous
The faith interests badges on profiles aren't clickable — tapping one could take you to that category's testimonies, which would make them useful rather than decorative
Home page

The "Stone of the Day" featured testimony has no indication of when it was last updated — if it hasn't changed in days, users stop checking it
The daily faith declaration is read-only — letting users tap to "declare it" with a brief animation (similar to the Amen burst) would make it feel interactive and personal
Community

There's no comment count visible on testimony cards in the feed — users have no signal that a testimony has active conversation before they tap in
No "Trending" or "Most Encouraged This Week" section to surface the best content to new users
Admin

The Support tab is built but there's no in-app notification or badge when new messages arrive — admins have no reason to check it unless they remember
Technical polish

The app doesn't remember your scroll position when you navigate back from a testimony detail — you start at the top of the community feed every time, which is frustrating
Push notification opt-in is triggered by the bell icon but there's no explanation of what you'll receive — a one-line tooltip or small prompt would improve the opt-in rate
